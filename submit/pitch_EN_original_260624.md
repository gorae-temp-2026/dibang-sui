Dibang turns the world's oldest credit system, the gift economy, into Moi Credit: a privacy-preserving, on-chain score — built to unlock uncollateralized loans on Sui.

Dibang makes your relationships your credit. We turn real-world social ties — the gifts, guestbooks and introductions of everyday life — into Moi Credit, an on-chain, privacy-preserving credit score. We don't store who you are; we store who you've been related to.

Why relationships? Every year, tens of trillions of won (~$25B) move through Korea's 222K weddings and 358K funerals as cash "relationship money" (부조) — and every culture has its own gift economy. It's the richest signal of who you can trust, yet it's all cash: untracked, un-credited, invisible to finance.

Dibang is a live product — a digital wedding invitation, guestbook and cash-gift flow already used at real weddings. Every interaction becomes a relationship signal.
Under the hood — a four-layer pipeline, grounded in category theory and a taxonomy of human relationships:

Layer 1 — Classification (taxonomy). Every relationship event is typed by a taxonomy of event types, action types and role pairs — the raw alphabet of social interaction.
Projection (functor). A projection functor maps each (event × action × role) into a relational signal across the four relational models of human interaction — Communal Sharing, Equality Matching, Authority Ranking, Market Pricing.

Layer 2 — Dynamics (trust ledgers). Signals of the same kind are folded — order-independent, like an abelian group — into relational ledgers: matrices of trust edges (giving / connection) and node-level fulfilment (repayment).

Layer 3 — Credit (Φ). Ledgers are normalized and propagated through the network by matrix power iteration — a reversed-giving PageRank in the EigenTrust lineage. Identity and associativity let these compose as a category; by Yoneda's lemma, a node is determined by its pattern of relationships, not its identity — which is exactly what makes it privacy-preserving.

Layer 4 — Integration. The relational categories are unified into a single trust network and resolved into Moi Credit: one absolute, on-chain score — your position in a living trust network, not the size of any balance.

On Sui, relationships are shared objects and Moi Credit is a composable Move object that can be read anonymously — without ever learning who you are.
